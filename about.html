🌿 Our Story

A Digital Revolution in Conservation

RootNet was born out of a critical need to protect Sri Lanka's rapidly diminishing forest cover and endangered species. Traditional methods of conservation were struggling to keep pace with modern threats such as illegal logging, poaching, and climate-induced wildfires.

In collaboration with the Sri Lankan Forest Department, we developed RootNet as an advanced digital ecosystem. By integrating cutting-edge IoT sensors, satellite imagery, AI-powered analytics, and real-time alerts, we've created a comprehensive platform that empowers rangers, scientists, and policymakers to act swiftly and decisively.

🌿 Preserving Heritage Since 2024
🎯
Our Mission
To provide actionable, real-time intelligence for the protection of Sri Lanka's forest ecosystems. We strive to bridge the gap between technology and nature, empowering conservationists with the tools they need to monitor biodiversity, prevent environmental disasters, and combat illegal activities.
👁️
Our Vision
A future where every hectare of Sri Lanka's natural forests is digitally protected, ensuring that wildlife thrives, ecosystems remain balanced, and future generations inherit a rich, green, and biologically diverse island. We envision expanding our smart networks across all vital eco-zones.
⏳ Journey

Our Milestones

2024

The Foundation

RootNet is officially proposed to the Sri Lankan Forest Department. Initial pilot projects utilizing basic IoT sensors are deployed in the Sinharaja Forest Reserve.

2025

AI Integration & Expansion

Implementation of AI-powered fire detection and automated wildlife tracking. The network expands to include Horton Plains and Uda Walawe National Park.

2026

Nationwide Smart Grid

RootNet becomes the central intelligence platform for all protected forests in Sri Lanka, achieving a 40% reduction in illegal logging incidents and saving thousands of hectares from wildfires.
